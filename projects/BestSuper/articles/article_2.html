Does your super have any hidden talents? Find out.

8th of May, 2016 | Christopher Martin

Aside from being a fantastic avenue to save for your later years in retirement, superannuation also provides you with a lot of benefits which you perhaps had not known about. For example, contributing to your superannuation is an easy way to pay less tax every financial year. If you set up a salary sacrifice agreement with your current place of employment, you arrange to exchange the income tax rate you normally pay every year for a much more stylish super contributions tax rate, which is 15% less.

Another hidden talent that is always lurking around a superannuation plan is cheaper insurance. Seeing as though a lot of super funds in fact purchase insurances in large quantities, they have the leeway to offer cheaper deals with common insurers. In which case, this creates insurance covers that are substantially cheaper when purchased via a super fund than other independent insurance companies.

The final sneaky, little-known benefit/talent of today's modern superannuation super funds is that they protect you largely from going brankrupt once you retire. If you have your savings inside of a super fund that is regulated by the government, in most cases this means that your money inside this fund is protected from creditors in the unfortunate event that you may need to file for bankruptcy. View this as a sort of backup plan, incase any unforeseen circumstances may become of your small business/start up. You can sleep well knowing that your finances are kept safe.
