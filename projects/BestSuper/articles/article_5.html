Super makes up 10% of your salary, see how.

16th of April, 2017 | Jim Halpert

That’s a significant amount in anyone’s language. And when you consider that the average person has around $45,000 in their super by their mid-to-late 30s, it makes good sense to think about super as more than just a deduction on your payslip.

Your employer must pay 9.5% of your salary into a super fund. This is called the Super Guarantee and it's the law. The Super Guarantee will gradually increase to 12% in coming years.
